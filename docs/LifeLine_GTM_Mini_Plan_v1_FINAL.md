## 1. GTM objective

Validate that users actively rely on LifeLine during real-world safety situations and establish repeat weekly usage. Success after first 90 days post-V2 launch:

- 2,000–5,000 registered users
- ≥ 40% Day-30 retention
- ≥ 60% of users complete one safety action weekly
- 1,000+ trusted contacts added across the network
- Alert delivery < 5 seconds
- False alert rate < 10%

## 2. Positioning

> *For students, commuters, and individuals who move alone, LifeLine is a personal safety companion that lets you instantly notify trusted people and escalate emergencies — in one tap.*

**The real pain isn't "people need another messaging app." It's that when people travel alone or feel unsafe, existing tools (WhatsApp share-location, manual phone calls) require too many steps and nobody knows their real-time status.**

LifeLine sits in the gap between *"text a friend"* (slow, manual, no escalation) and *"call the police"* (slow to connect, no location share, no follow-through).

## 3. Beachhead market

**Phase 1: University students — female undergraduates in Lagos and Nsukka.**

Why this segment first:

- Strong, daily safety concerns (validated in discovery)
- Existing tight-knit communities (hostels, departments, course-mates)
- High referral behaviour (one student tells five)
- Heavy smartphone usage on campuses
- Faster feedback cycles than working professionals

**Initial campuses:** University of Lagos · Lagos State University · University of Nigeria Nsukka

**Primary message:** *Never walk alone — even when you're physically alone.*

## 4. Messaging framework

| Audience | Pain | Message |
|---|---|---|
| Students | Night movement after lectures | *"Heading home? Let someone watch your journey."* |
| Women generally | Safety anxiety in transit | *"Help without drawing attention."* |
| Workers | Late commutes | *"One tap keeps people informed."* |
| Parents | Child safety | *"Know they arrived safely."* |

**Tagline:** *Safety in one tap. Fast, private, and reliable safety communication during uncertain moments.*

## 5. Acquisition strategy

### Campus Ambassador Programme

- **Recruit:** student influencers · department reps · hostel leaders
- **Reward:** referral bonuses · leaderboards · merchandise · recognition
- **Target:** 50 ambassadors in Phase 1
- **Expected:** each ambassador acquires 30–50 users → 1,500–2,500 users organically

### Referral Loop (built into the product)

The trusted-contact mechanic IS a referral loop. When a user adds a contact, the contact receives: *"Ada added you as a safety contact. Download LifeLine."* Each active user potentially invites 3–5 others.

### Social Content Strategy

Short relatable videos on TikTok, Instagram, X, YouTube:

- *"POV: You're entering a ride at 10pm"*
- *"POV: You forgot to text your parents"*
- *"Walking from class alone"*

Frequency: 3–5 posts weekly. Mix user stories, safety tips, scenario demos.

## 6. Launch sequence

### Soft Launch (Weeks 1–4 post-V2)

- **Audience:** 300–500 users — campus communities, friends, early adopters
- **Goals:** test notification reliability · validate onboarding · measure check-in usage
- **Track:** session completion · contact additions · false alerts

### Public Launch (Weeks 5–12 post-V2)

- **Channels:** student communities · WhatsApp groups · micro-influencers · campus events
- **Campaign:** `#ArriveSafeChallenge` — users post *"Reached home safely"* with a screenshot
- **Prizes:** data bundles · transport vouchers · merchandise

## 7. Partnerships (lighter, not police-first)

We deliberately avoid police partnerships in Phase 1 (operational complexity, slow institutional sign-off). Instead, lighter partnerships that compound trust and reach:

- **Universities** — orientation programmes, student welfare initiatives
- **Ride-hailing communities** — Bolt and Uber driver associations
- **Campus shuttle associations** — shared safety messaging
- **NGOs focused on women's safety** — credibility plus community access

## 8. Pricing

**Launch with Freemium.**

| Tier | Price | What's included |
|---|---|---|
| **Free** | ₦0 | Trusted contacts (up to 5) · Basic alerts · Check-ins · Panic mode |
| **Premium** | ₦1,000–₦1,500 / month | Unlimited location history · Family circles · Longer tracking · Advanced analytics |

**Why freemium first:** Habit formation matters more than monetisation in Year 1. Avoid charging until daily-habit behaviour is proven.

## 9. Activation funnel

```
Install → OTP signup → Add trusted contact → First check-in
       → First emergency simulation → Weekly habit
```

**Key onboarding constraint:** Do not let users leave setup without (a) adding at least 1 trusted contact, AND (b) completing a test safety check. This prevents the "downloaded but never used" trap that kills safety apps.

## 10. Retention plan

- **Weekly reminders:** *"Travelling tonight? Start a safety session."*
- **Behaviour triggers:** late evening movement · missed check-ins · weekend travel
- **Gamification:** badges for 10 Safe Arrivals · Safety Streak · Trusted Guardian

## 11. KPI dashboard (track weekly)

| Category | Metrics |
|---|---|
| Acquisition | App installs · Cost per acquisition · Referral conversion |
| Engagement | Weekly Active Users · Check-ins/week · Trusted contacts added |
| Safety | Alert success rate · Avg acknowledgment time · Escalation completion |
| Retention | Day-7 retention · Day-30 retention |

## 12. Biggest GTM risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Users forget app exists | Reminder notifications + scheduled check-ins |
| False panic triggers | 3-second hold + 2:45 grace cancellation period |
| Notification failures | SMS backup via Termii (V2) |
| Privacy concerns | Clear onboarding explanations + consent flow |
| Users don't add contacts | Forced trusted-contact setup at onboarding |

## 13. First 90-day execution roadmap

- **Month 1:** MVP closed beta · recruit ambassadors · onboard first 300 users
- **Month 2:** Referral campaigns · social content push · campus outreach
- **Month 3:** Partnerships (universities, ride-hail communities) · retention optimisation · analytics review

## 14. Gaps in this plan (honest disclosure)

This mini-plan is condensed from the team's full 13-section GTM plan. The following gaps remain and are flagged for the team to address before public launch (V4):

1. **CAC and unit economics are not modelled.** The ambassador programme assumes 50 ambassadors × 30–50 users each, but the cost per user (data bundles, merchandise, prize incentives) hasn't been costed. Risk: programme blows budget at scale.
2. **Premium tier pricing (₦1,000–1,500/month) is a guess.** No willingness-to-pay research done. Recommend a Van Westendorp price-sensitivity survey before charging.
3. **No localisation plan for Yoruba/Igbo/Hausa.** Current MVP is English-only. For mass adoption beyond university campuses, multi-language onboarding is required.
4. **Partnership pipeline is named but not contacted.** Bolt driver community, NGOs, university welfare offices listed but no warm intros documented. Need a partnership owner with a 30-day outreach target.
5. **Crisis response protocol is unspecified.** What happens when a real SOS results in real harm? Who from LifeLine speaks to the press? To the user's family? To the police? This is a sensitive policy area for any safety product.
6. **Competitive response unmodelled.** What happens if Bolt adds in-app SOS, or WhatsApp ships a "safety mode"? No defensive moat plan beyond Nigerian localisation.
7. **No retention experiment backlog.** Retention plan lists tactics (badges, reminders) but no A/B test prioritisation. Recommend RICE-scoring 5–7 retention experiments for Q2 post-launch.
8. **No paid-acquisition channel testing.** Plan is 100% organic. Even a small Meta Ads test (₦50K) would establish a baseline CAC for comparison against organic.
9. **App store optimisation is not addressed.** Title, screenshots, keywords, ratings management — all unspecified.
10. **No customer support model.** When a user can't add a contact or the OTP fails, who responds? Inbox? WhatsApp? Twitter? Currently undefined.

> *These gaps don't block the capstone submission — they're a backlog for the GTM team to work through during Y1 Q1–Q2 (post-V2 launch).*
